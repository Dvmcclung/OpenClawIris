## May 28 PM Summary (full entries)

### The Retention Cliff / 22x Story Memory (5-28 PM, Marketing LTB 2026)
Facts 5–10% retention raw; ~67% in story structure. Default structure: number → story context → implication. Never: number → number → number. IMfA: YES.

### Multi-Audience Data Storytelling / "Impossible to Ignore" Test (5-28 PM, ThoughtSpot 2026)
Segment audience before format decisions. Executive frame: "What does this mean for the business?" Pre-write: "What decision is this reader making?" Connect numbers to executive-relevant stakes. IMfA: YES.

### Reporting Friction / Decision Clarity (5-28 PM, Wharton/Mendler 2026)
Best report = least cognitive work to act on. Score for "decision friction." If key finding not visible in first 10 seconds, report fails primary function regardless of analytical quality. IMfA: YES.

### 13-Stakeholder Enterprise Narrative Architecture (5-28 PM, Pedowitz/Forrester 2026)
Average B2B purchase involves 13 internal + 9 external stakeholders. Structure: core narrative + stakeholder-segmented messaging modules sharing same spine. CEO frame: business risk; SC VP frame: operational execution — same facts, different emphasis. IMfA: YES.

### Clarity Sprint / Three Alignment Questions (5-28 PM, Forbes/Frye 2026)
Pre-write diagnostic: (1) Who is your ideal customer? (2) What one thing should they believe? (3) What does success look like in 90 days? Different answers = clarity problem in every piece produced. IMfA: YES.

### Narrow vs. Wide Decisions — Report Misclassification (5-28 PM, MIT SMR May 2026)
Narrow decisions (clear data, measurable outcomes) → recommendation-forward. Wide decisions (contested goals, alignment matters) → deliberation is the deliverable. Treating wide as narrow = polished analysis that fails to drive action. Add section: "What this analysis does NOT resolve." IMfA: YES.

### Three-Process Narrative Structure (5-29 PM, Penn LPS/McKinsey April 2026)
Staging (establish stakes) → Plot progression (build tension via challenge) → Cognitive tension (resolution demanding decision/action). McKinsey: storytelling = #1 foundational skill leaders need this decade. Coaching signal: Does it establish stakes before presenting data? Does conflict appear before resolution? ### Strategic Clarity as #1 Executive Performance Constraint (5-29 PM, LHH 2026 C-Suite Report)
>25% of C-suite leaders cite lack of strategic clarity as top limiter — above AI skill gaps and talent retention. Unclear reports ARE the clarity gap executives report as their primary bottleneck. Add "Decision Frame" sentence at top of every report: "This is a [narrow/wide] decision. The recommendation is [actionable now / requires stakeholder alignment]." IMfA: YES.

Effective storytelling bridges logic and emotion simultaneously — not layered on top of data. Structural test: does the story exist to support the argument, or does the argument exist to justify the story? Former = persuasion; latter = entertainment. Volume-based content strategy has collapsed. Audiences now select for authenticity, expertise, connection. Strongest communicators win through narrative coherence, not frequency. Coaching: "Does this piece fit the same identity as the last three posts? Would a reader get an accurate sense of who Dale is from this alone?" IMfA: YES.

### AI Summarization Raises the Bar for Human Reports / 30-Second Test (6-01 PM, Mendler/Wharton May 2026)
82% of enterprise leaders use generative AI weekly. AI summaries are now the implicit benchmark. Human reports that take 10 minutes to reach their point lose the comparison. Standard: (1) decision statement in first paragraph, (2) recommendation before evidence, (3) executive summary that is actually a summary. Apply AI-summary test: if an AI summarized this, would the summary contain decision, recommendation, and key risk? IMfA: YES.

## June 2 PM Summary (full entries)

### Narrative Architecture vs. Bullet Architecture / 4-S Structure (6-02 PM, WinningPresentations.com May 2026)
Bullet lists are processed as *lists to absorb*; narrative is processed as *a request to choose*. 4-S Framework: Setup (current state, one sentence) → Stakes (what is at risk if nothing changes) → Shift (the one recommended action) → Stake-out (what changes at yes vs. no). Executive case study: 31-slide deck deferred, same data in 8 narrative slides approved. Coaching: Does the document give the reader two futures to compare? If both futures aren't visible, the decision can't be made — reader will defer. IMfA: YES.

### Context-Aware Communication / "Context-Aware" Upgrades Personalization (6-02 PM, MHC Automation May 2026)
Leading communicators in 2026 build audience models *before* writing — understanding what decisions the audience is currently navigating, what risks they're carrying, and what would make their job easier today. Context-aware = adapting to where the reader is in a decision journey, not just demographics. "Help embedded across the journey": best documents anticipate the reader's next question and answer it in the same breath. IMfA: YES.

### Findings vs. Analysis Separation / The Third-Party Test (6-02 PM, ASK Training May 2026)
Conflating findings with analysis degrades clarity: "Can this sentence be verified by a third party who has access to the same data?" If yes = finding; if no = analysis. Mixed sentences should be flagged and split — one fact sentence, one interpretation sentence. The fix is simple; the credibility gain is disproportionate to the effort. Senior reader test: understand situation + required action without reading past the executive summary. IMfA: YES.
