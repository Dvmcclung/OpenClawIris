### Thought Leadership Quality Gap Is Exploitable — 17%/75% Rule (2026-04-07)

Hidden buyers (55% of decision-makers who never attend sales calls) are actively searching for quality and finding mostly noise — the rare quality practitioner voice wins consideration before any sales conversation starts.

### SCQA + MECE — The Complete Pyramid Architecture for Analytical Reports (2026-04-09)
Minto Pyramid operationalized with two new named tools: SCQA (Situation → Complication → Question → Answer) as the narrative entry priming sequence that makes BLUF feel inevitable rather than abrupt; MECE (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive) as the two-part test for argument structure — no overlap (same evidence under multiple arguments), no gaps (obvious questions left unanswered). Full architecture: SCQA entry + BLUF conclusion + MECE supporting arguments. "Think bottom up, communicate top down." SCQA also works for LinkedIn posts: Situation (what the reader knows about supply chain AI) → Complication (what has changed) → Question (what to think differently about) → Answer (Dale's POV).

### Audience-First Narrative Arcs — Knowledge State Drives Structure, Not Content Type (2026-04-10)
Narrative arc format should be selected based on the audience's knowledge state, not the data's complexity. Four-dimension pre-structural assessment: (1) knowledge level; (2) time availability; (3) quantitative comfort; (4) desired outcome. Standard arc (hook → context → process → insight → impact → resolution → future) for unfamiliar topics. Inverted arc (BLUF) for expert audiences with time pressure. Upstream gate before SCQA: establish audience's knowledge state first. For Dale's LinkedIn: supply chain executives = high domain knowledge + low time availability = inverted arc default. (Turning Data Into Wisdom, January 2025)

### Pyramid Principle as Architecture Rule — "Every Section Leads With Its Main Point" (2026-04-10)
Common misapplication: using Pyramid Principle as a delivery rule (BLUF the opening) while leaving supporting structure bottom-up. Full architectural application: every section leads with its conclusion before its supporting evidence. MECE diagnostic applied retroactively: (1) does any evidence appear under more than one argument? (2) Is there a question the reader would ask that no argument addresses? Psychological resistance explained: "building to a conclusion feels rigorous because it mirrors analytical process" — but it is analyst-level, not executive-level. That naming of the resistance is a coaching-ready formulation.
