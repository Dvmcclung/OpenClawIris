**June 10 PM (3 insights):**
1. **Story as Evidence, Not Warmth — 90-Second Rule / Evidence vs. Texture Classification (6-10 PM, Planning in High Heels/Chip Heath/Flowtrace)**: Past 90 seconds, story stops proving the argument and starts being the argument. Two story types: evidence-type (proves a specific claim — belongs in body) vs. context-type (orients the reader — belongs in introduction/staging). Written standard: narrative example should connect back to its analytical claim within ~250 words (~90 seconds). New coaching signal: "evidence or texture?" test. IMfA: YES.
2. **Audience Segmentation: 2–3 Stakeholder Groups Is the Structural Maximum (6-10 PM, Moxie Institute/Army Research Institute/OECD)**: Audience analysis is a *structural decision*, not a pre-writing thought exercise. 2–3 distinct stakeholder groups; each needs a calibrated section. More than three and the document fragments; fewer than two and it's demographic profiling, not decision-context mapping. Exec summary = segment 1 (strategic decision-maker); body sections = segment 2 (operational evaluator); appendices = segment 3 (technical validator). 3–4x impact multiplier for rigorous audience analysis. New coaching signal: "2–3 segment audit." IMfA: YES.
3. **Visual Structure Is a Clarity Mechanism, Not Formatting Preference (6-10 PM, LiveGroup/Bruner/Uri Hasson)**: 22x memorability (Bruner); 63% recall for story-led content (Stanford/Heath). Neural coupling (Hasson/Princeton): structured narrative synchronizes reader's brain with writer's. Visual structure is the manifestation of narrative architecture — headers/white space reduce extraneous load, freeing cognitive capacity for analytical content. The "shape test": can the reader navigate without reading every word? If not, the document lacks the shape that enables retention. IMfA: YES.
