### "Outcomes Over Understanding" — The Root Cognitive Reframe Behind BLUF (2026-04-01)
Unwritten (March 2026) + Pryor Learning (March 2026): academic training rewards demonstrating reasoning; executive communication demands the opposite — conclusion first, then evidence, then method if needed. Concrete test: "Is my first sentence a demonstration of thinking (what I've been working on) or a statement of impact (what this means for you)?" The 7 C's framework's most under-applied element is Concreteness — abstract writing forces the reader to translate, adding cognitive load. Clarity is a credibility mechanism: when readers understand quickly, they assume the writer understood fully; when they have to work to extract meaning, they attribute the effort to the writer's confusion. In an AI-saturated environment, clarity has become a trust signal, not just a convenience.
IMfA nominated: MAYBE — supporting insight for the BLUF/Smart Brevity cluster, not standalone.

### The 7 C's Framework — Operationalizing "Clarity" as Predictability of Next Action (2026-04-03)
Pryor Learning (March 2026) synthesis: 7 C's — Clarity, Conciseness, Correctness, Completeness, Consideration, Courtesy, Concreteness. ### Executive Presence Traps — When Confidence Suppresses Information (2026-04-03)
HBR (April 2026): at senior levels, the behaviors that signal competence in individual contributors (having answers, projecting confidence, improving others' ideas) backfire because authority is large enough to suppress dissent and limit information flow. Three named traps: (1) having the answers signals thinking is done; (2) projecting confidence creates a credibility investment that makes course-correction costly; (3) jumping in to improve ideas shuts down exploration. Shift required: "prove you're right → help others do their best thinking." Written implication: documents that present conclusions as fully settled can produce the same damage as declarative speech. The strongest senior-level communication may need to be structured as thinking-in-progress, not conclusions-for-ratification.
IMfA nominated: YES.

### "What Decision Should This Inform?" — The Upstream Pre-Writing Gate (2026-04-06)
Named as a formalized pre-writing question (upstream of structure, not a revision rule): ask before drafting, not during review. Function: a decision-gate. If the writer cannot answer it, the document is not ready to write — only ready to gather more information. When answered, structure becomes obvious (open with decision context → lead with the finding that bears on it → close with specific next step). Organizational version: crisis-management credibility is built through "alignment before pressure arrives" — clear decision authority, escalation paths, and shared definition of "good" before stress hits. Credibility formula for difficult-period updates: specific action taken + credible timeline + update cadence. (Unwritten March 2026; S&A Communications March 2026)
IMfA nominated: YES.
