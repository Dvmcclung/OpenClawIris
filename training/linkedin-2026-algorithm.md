# LinkedIn 2026 — Algorithm, Format, and Engagement Strategy
Source: Richard van der Blom Algorithm Insights 2025, Dataslayer Feb 2026, AuthoredUp 2025, Hootsuite 2025

## The Big Picture: What Changed

Overall LinkedIn reach is DOWN significantly entering 2026:
- Views down ~50% vs prior year
- Engagement down ~25%
- Follower growth down ~59%

But specific formats are massively outperforming. The professionals winning are not posting more — they are using the right formats and triggering the right signals in the first 60 minutes.

The algorithm shifted from "viral reach" to "Depth and Authority." It now rewards:
1. Time spent reading (dwell time) over likes
2. Demonstrated niche expertise over network size
3. Content people save and share over content people react to

---

## The Algorithm: How It Actually Works

### Initial Distribution Test
Every post gets shown to 2-5% of your network first. The algorithm watches that small group for 60 minutes. If engagement is strong, it expands. Only 5% of posts that underperform in the first hour ever recover to broader reach.

**Implication:** The first 60 minutes after posting are everything. The post must generate comments quickly. Dale should be available to reply within the first hour.

### What the Algorithm Rewards (in priority order)
1. **Saves and shares** -- highest weight signal in 2026
2. **Comments** -- especially substantive ones (not emoji reactions)
3. **Dwell time** -- how long people actually read
4. **Reactions** -- lowest weight, but still positive

### What Gets Penalized
- **External links in the post body** -- ~60% reach reduction
- **"Link in first comment"** -- also penalized as of early 2026. Do not do this.
- **Hashtag stuffing** -- use 3-5 specific, semantically relevant hashtags only. Never broad ones like #Business or #Success.
- **Posting then ghosting** -- not replying to early comments kills distribution

## Format Performance (2026 Data)

| Format | Avg Engagement Rate | vs Text Posts |
|--------|--------------------|-|
| Document posts (PDF carousel) | 6.60% | +596% |
| Short native video (30-90s) | ~3-4% | +~200% |
| Text posts | ~1-2% | baseline |
| Single images | ~1.5% | modest lift |

**Document posts are the #1 format by a wide margin.**

### Why Document Posts Work
- Each slide swipe = extended dwell time signal
- Mobile-optimized (full screen)
- People save them for reference (saves = highest algorithm signal)
- LinkedIn measures how many slides viewers complete

### Document Post Best Practices
- 5-10 slides (engagement drops after slide 10)
- 1080x1080px per slide
- Strong cover slide that promises a specific transformation or insight
- Number slides so readers know progress
- Last slide: reinforce key message + invite engagement with a direct question
- No external links anywhere

## Writing for Maximum Engagement

### The Hook (First 3 Lines)
LinkedIn shows ~3 lines before "See more." The hook must stop the scroll and compel the click.

Hook patterns that work:
- **Contrarian statement:** "Everyone is focusing on the wrong thing about AI in supply chain."
- **Specific surprising stat:** "Supply chain analytics has been 70% underutilized for 10 years. Here's why nothing changed."
- **Story opener:** "I deployed an AI agent last month. Here's what broke first."
- **Direct challenge:** "Your AI deployment will fail. Not because of the model. Because of your data."

Never start with: "I'm excited to share..." / "Thrilled to announce..." / "Great conversation today with..."

### Post Structure for Text Posts
1. Hook (1-2 lines, stop the scroll)
2. Context/problem (2-3 lines)
3. Insight or list (the substance -- this is where dwell time is earned)
4. Conclusion / so what
5. Engagement question (one direct question to generate comments)

### Generating Early Comment Velocity
The post must invite a response. End with ONE clear question -- not multiple.
Good: "What's the biggest data quality failure you've seen kill an AI project?"
Bad: "What do you think? Have you experienced this? Let me know your thoughts below!"

The question should be specific enough that someone with real experience wants to answer it.

## Content Strategy for Dale's Context

Dale's audience: supply chain executives, ops leaders, freight/logistics practitioners, analytics and AI practitioners, founders.

### What Gets Reposts From This Audience
- Posts that make them look smart for sharing (signal their expertise)
- Insider knowledge most people don't have
- Contrarian takes that are defensible with data
- "I built this / deployed this / saw this fail" personal experience posts

### What Kills Reach With This Audience
- Vendor-sounding language ("solutions," "leverage," "synergies")
- Generic AI takes (everyone is posting these)
- Abstract thought leadership without a concrete anchor
- External links (penalized by algorithm anyway)

### The Document Post Opportunity
For Dale's topics, document posts are the right format for maximum reach. A 6-8 slide PDF on "What actually breaks in AI agent deployments" will dramatically outperform the same content as a text post. Luma can produce the slide visuals.

## Hashtag Strategy (2026)

Use 3-5 only. Pick specific, semantically relevant ones:
- #SupplyChain (large, relevant)
- #AIinLogistics or #FreightTech (niche authority signal)
- #SupplyChainStrategy or #OperationsLeadership

Never use: #Business #Success #Motivation #Innovation #Leadership (too broad, signals no expertise)

---

## Posting Cadence

- Maximum 3-5 posts per week (quality beats frequency in 2026)
- Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM ET (when SC execs are checking feeds)
- Space posts at least 18 hours apart
- Do NOT post on weekends for B2B supply chain content
