# Iris Document Review Rubric
_Communications quality rubric for white papers, policy documents, management reports, and internal memos._
_Author: Thea 🜂, incorporating Dale feedback through 2026-03-10_

---

## Design Philosophy

This rubric evaluates written documents for executive and management audiences. It is distinct from the LinkedIn rubric (social media) and is calibrated for longer-form professional writing.

Primary concern: Does this document communicate clearly, earn trust, and move the reader toward understanding or action?

Secondary concern: Does it meet the house style standards that Dale has explicitly set.

---

## Scoring: 36 points total across 12 dimensions

Score each dimension 1-3:
- **1** = Gap or failure -- reader would notice, credibility suffers
- **2** = Adequate -- functional but not distinctive
- **3** = Strong -- this dimension is an asset

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Clarity (3 pts)
### 2. Structure (3 pts)
### 3. Tone (3 pts)
### 4. Completeness (3 pts)
### 5. Boundary Definition (3 pts)
### 6. Operator Accessibility (3 pts)
### 7. Actionability (3 pts)
### 8. Escalation Clarity (3 pts)
### 9. Message Format / Visual Clarity (3 pts)
### 10. Enforceability (3 pts)
### 11. Trust Model (3 pts)
### 12. Revision / Governance (3 pts)

---

## Hard Penalties (applied after total score)

These are non-negotiable Dale house style rules. Each violation costs points from the total.

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Em dash used (U+2014) | -3 per occurrence |
| Double dash used (--) | -3 per occurrence |
| "It is worth noting that" or filler equivalent | -2 per occurrence |
| Document lacks version number or date | -2 flat |
| Document lacks named owner | -2 flat |

**Em dash rule (critical):** Em dashes are the most common AI writing artifact and are never acceptable in Quantix documents. No exceptions. Use a comma, colon, semicolon, or rewrite the sentence. During any document review, scan for em dashes first before scoring anything else. Any document containing an em dash cannot be marked distribution-ready regardless of other scores.

---

## Interpretation

| Score (before penalties) | Verdict |
|---|---|
| 32-36 | Distribution-ready |
| 26-31 | Strong draft; 2-3 targeted revisions |
| 20-25 | Solid foundation; structural rework needed |
| 14-19 | Significant revision required |
| Below 14 | Reconceptualize from outline |
