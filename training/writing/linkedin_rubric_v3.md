# LinkedIn Rubric v3 — Repost-Optimized
_Developed by Iris, 2026-03-03. Based on Anett Grant frameworks + LinkedIn reach mechanics._

## Design Philosophy

v2 optimized for quality reads. v3 optimizes for social amplification. Those are different goals.

People don't repost posts — they repost ideas. A repost is a public identity statement: "I want my network to see this." The rubric must score for that behavior, not just for clarity and credibility.

Key shift: **Influence, don't educate.** (Anett Grant)

---

## Scoring

**Total: 70 points + AI penalty**
**Repost target: 55+/70**

---

### Hook (10 pts)
First 2-3 lines earn the "see more" click.

- **1-3:** Generic opener, could be any post by anyone
- **4-6:** Creates tension or curiosity but doesn't fully commit
- **7-9:** Stops the scroll with a bold claim or unexpected framing
- **10:** Reader has to know where this goes — no choice but to click

---

### Core Claim Clarity (10 pts)
One sharp, defensible thesis that the whole post orbits. Focus over density — one unforgettable point beats five good ones.

- **1-3:** Collection of loosely related observations, no center of gravity
- **4-6:** Main idea exists but is buried or diluted by supporting points
- **7-9:** Clear position, well-supported
- **10:** Single sentence states a clear position; everything else is evidence; reader could summarize the post in one line

---

### Opinion Strength / Voice (15 pts)
Does the post take a real position someone would want to amplify? This is the primary repost driver.

- **1-5:** Presents information without a stance — "on one hand, on the other hand"
- **6-9:** Has a point of view but hedges or softens to avoid friction
- **10-13:** Clear conviction, specific claim, practitioner voice throughout
- **14-15:** Makes a specific, arguable claim with full commitment — someone who disagrees would respond; someone who agrees would share immediately

---

### Narrative Arc (10 pts)
Tension → pivot → payoff. Grant's story formula: setup, conflict, resolution, relevance.

- **1-3:** Analytical essay — facts and observations with no narrative shape
- **4-6:** Some tension established but resolution is flat or generic
- **7-9:** Clear structure — reader feels it without seeing it
- **10:** Setup (common belief / the situation), conflict (why it's wrong or incomplete), payoff (what it actually means) — the structure creates investment and the resolution delivers the shareable moment

---

### Challenge Factor (10 pts)
Does it push back on common wisdom, status quo, or default assumptions? Grant: "appeal, don't please."

- **1-3:** Confirms what the reader already believes
- **4-6:** Introduces a nuance but doesn't challenge the underlying assumption
- **7-9:** Names a common assumption and argues against it with evidence
- **10:** Directly confronts a widely-held belief — not to provoke, but because the evidence points somewhere most people aren't looking

---

### Repost Identity Fit (10 pts)
Would sharing this post enhance a practitioner's professional identity in their network?

- **1-3:** Too niche (only specialists care) or too generic (everyone already knows this)
- **4-6:** Relevant to the audience but not distinctive enough to signal expertise
- **7-9:** Specific enough to demonstrate real domain knowledge, broad enough to have wide relevance
- **10:** A supply chain leader, ops exec, or thoughtful generalist would gain social capital by amplifying it — sharing it says "I think at this level"

---

### LinkedIn Mechanics (5 pts)
Format hygiene. Table stakes — doesn't drive reposts but failure here kills reach.

- **1-2:** Wall of text, header-dependent, desktop-only
- **3-4:** Readable, mostly clean, minor issues
- **5:** Short paragraphs, mobile-readable, no structural crutches, closes with an opinionated statement or a question worth sitting with

---

### AI Smell (penalty: -5 to 0)
Does it trigger "written by AI" instincts?

- **0:** No red flags — reads as a practitioner, not a content machine
- **-2:** Minor patterns (hedging language, over-structured transitions, mild anaphora)
- **-5:** Obvious tells (em dashes, listicle headers, "it is worth noting," generic CTA, four-item lists)

**Dale's hard rules (automatic -3 each if violated):**
- No em dashes
- No double dashes
- No listicle headers

---

## v2 → v3 Changes

| v2 | v3 | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Insight Density | Core Claim Clarity | Focus over density; one unforgettable point beats five good ones |
| Audience Fit | Repost Identity Fit | Reads metric → reposts metric; adds social capital dimension |
| Quotability | Folded into Opinion Strength | Quotability is a symptom of strong opinions, not a separate skill |
| Human Signal | Opinion Strength / Voice (15 pts) | Elevated weight; primary repost driver |
| — | Narrative Arc (10 pts) | New — Grant's story formula applied |
| — | Challenge Factor (10 pts) | New — Grant's "appeal not please" |
