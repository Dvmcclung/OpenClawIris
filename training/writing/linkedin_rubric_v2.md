# LinkedIn Post Rubric v2

## Scoring Dimensions

| Dimension | What it measures | 1 | 5 | 10 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hook | Does the first 2-3 lines earn the "see more" click? | Generic opener | Creates tension or curiosity | Stops the scroll with something unexpected or bold |
| Insight density | Does the reader learn something real per paragraph? | Vague generalities | One strong idea buried in filler | Every paragraph advances the argument |
| Audience fit | Would this resonate with a non-specialist in a corporate environment? | Too technical or too narrow | Clear but not compelling to outsiders | Accessible to anyone who manages people or systems |
| Quotability | Is there at least one sentence someone would screenshot or copy? | Nothing memorable | One decent line | Multiple lines that stand alone out of context |
| Human signal | Does it read like a practitioner wrote it from experience? | Consultant-speak, passive voice, listicle structure | Mostly natural with stiff moments | Opinionated, direct, occasionally surprising |
| LinkedIn mechanics | Is it structured for the platform? | Wall of text, header-dependent | Readable but could be tighter | Short paragraphs, works on mobile, no formatting crutches |
| AI smell (penalty) | Does it trigger "written by AI" instincts? | 0 = no red flags | minus 2 = minor patterns | minus 5 = obvious tells |

## Passing Threshold
- Hook 7+, Insight density 7+, Audience fit 7+, Quotability 7+, Human signal 7+, LinkedIn mechanics 6+, AI smell 0 penalty
- Aggregate target: 45+ out of 60

## Known AI tells to avoid
- "X things that Y" as a section header
- "corporate-grade" / "consumer-grade" framing used structurally
- Passive voice in the analysis sections
- Hedging language ("it is worth noting", "it is important to consider")
- Over-structured transitions ("First... Second... Third...")
- Ending with a generic call to action instead of a specific question or statement
- Em dashes, double dashes, or overly long sentences with multiple clauses

## Rules
- Never use em dashes or double dashes
- No listicle headers mid-post
- Every paragraph must earn its place
- Hook must create tension or a bold claim in lines 1-2
- At least one quotable line per post
- End with something opinionated or a question worth thinking about
